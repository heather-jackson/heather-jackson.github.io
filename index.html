    • ABOUT

    I am a final-year PhD student at Imperial College London in the Department of Medicine, Section of Paediatric Infectious Diseases. I am supervised by Dr. Myrsini Kaforou, Dr. Jethro Herberg and Prof. Mike Levin. My PhD is funded by the Wellcome Trust as part of the Wellcome Trust PhD Programme in Bioinformatics and Theoretical Systems Biology at Imperial College London.

    My PhD thesis focuses on using multi-omic datasets to understand and diagnose paediatric infectious diseases. I am interested in using bioinformatic approaches to identify novel biomarkers of infection, and to explore the heterogeneity in the host response to infection. My research is highly interdisciplinary, spanning aspects of statistics, machine learning, biology, bioinformatics and medicine.

    Prior to starting my PhD in 2018, I completed an MSc. in Bioinformatics and Theoretical Systems Biology at Imperial College London and an undergraduate degree in Biology at The University of Manchester.

    In my free time (pandemic permitting) I enjoy travelling, going to live music events, climbing, skiing, learning French and exploring what London has to offer.
